Wayne Wu

I am a Research Associate in the Department of Computer Science at the University of California, Los Angeles, working with Bolei Zhou. Previously, I served as a Research Scientist at Shanghai AI Lab, where I led the Virtual Human Group. I was also a Visiting Scholar at Nanyang Technological University, working with Chen Change Loy. In June 2022, I obtained my PhD in the Department of Computer Science and Technology at Tsinghua University.

Research

My research lies at the intersection of computer vision, computer graphics, and machine learning. My goal is to develop Human-centric Embodied AI Systems that promote urban mobility, safety, and accessibility, by addressing key challenges in scalability of agent learning environments, as well as realism and situationality of populated virtual humans. This effort is pursued through three primary directions:

  • Scalable Embodied AI Simulators: Building large-scale robot learning platforms with diverse assets and infinite urban scenes, and enabling high-efficiency training, as in MetaUrban, UrbanSim, Vid2Sim, and OmniObject3D.
  • Realistic Virtual Humans: Constructing high-fidelity 4D volumetric capture systems and datasets, as in DNA-Rendering and RenderMe-360; and developing human foundation models to obtain generalizable representations, as in CosmicMan and MotionBert.
  • Situational Motion Generation: Learning context-aware generative models of human motions and behaviors from diverse modalities -- including vision, audio, and language, as in PedGen, EmbodiedHuman, and Everybody's Talkin'.
